Using Desmos Graphs in Jupyter Notebooks

OK – I went another way in this article and got something to work.

Here’s the beginning of what was to be the article.

I recently finished Calculus I as the World’s Oldest Math Major. In class and on exams, I had the pleasure of competing with people a third my age in hunting and pecking on a TI-84 calculator.

In contrast, at home, I only spent as much time as I needed on that device to do well on exams. Instead, my preferred tools for calculation and graphing were a Python stack consisting of an open IPython terminal with SymPy installed and a browser opened to https://www.desmos.com/calculator for graphing.

I love SymPy for undergraduate math. It has everything I need to quickly try things out to refresh my memory, or to double-check the answers I found manually before submitting them to the online homework portal. Although I’ve learned enough of it to be dangerous, one tool that’s not a great fit for a busy semester is matplotlib, Python’s most popular plotting library. Although its website boasts that “Matplotlib makes easy things easy and hard things possible,” I find I can agree with only the latter part of that claim.

For spinning up a quick graph to check a result, for example, doing this in Matplotlib would take me probably five times as long as it would in Desmos. (If I did it all the time, I could probably cut that down to three times as long, but that’s still not great).

from sympy import symbols, Eq, latex
x, y = symbols("x y")
eq = Eq(y, x**2)
latex(eq)

'y = x^{2}'

from IPython.display import Javascript, display

script = """
    // Important, need to reset height or will not be visible.
    element.style.height = "500px"
    var calculator = Desmos.GraphingCalculator(element);
    console.log(calculator);
    calculator.setExpression({id:'graph1', latex:'y=x^2 +2'});
    calculator.setExpression({id:'graph2', latex: 'y=2x+1'});

"""

api_url = "https://www.desmos.com/api/v1.9/calculator.js?apiKey=dcb31709b452b1cf9dc26972add0fda6"
display(Javascript(script, lib=[api_url]))
